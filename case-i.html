A positive-action leadership programme for Black staff in further education.

Designed and delivered with the college and its programme consultant in response to sector-wide under-representation of Black, Asian and ethnically diverse leaders — with coaching at the heart of delivery.

§ 01 The context

An under-representation problem the sector has known about for years.

The college recognised that local and national data demonstrate clearly that Black staff are under-represented in senior leadership and management positions across the further education sector. The Association of Colleges estimates that between twelve and fourteen of England's 239 FE colleges are led by Black, Asian and ethnic-minority principals.

According to The Guardian (August 2020), the proportion of Black, Asian and ethnically diverse college leaders has dropped from 13% in 2017 to around 5% or 6% in 2020. By sharp comparison, Black, Asian and ethnically diverse students are estimated to form approximately 30% of the overall FE student population.

The client is determined to address this — fully committed, in its own words, to taking “practical and reasonable” steps to address under-representation through Leadership and Management Positive Action development programmes for Black staff.

§ 02 The brief

A first-year programme, designed in partnership.

2021 was the first year of running this programme. The college engaged a programme consultant and HDC to assist with the development and delivery of a thorough and high-quality intervention.

The initial challenge was clearing up the confusion about proactive, targeted, positive-action measures and clarifying its importance. Many college leaders and staff did not understand it — and in many cases thought it was illegal, confusing positive discrimination with positive action.

Beyond that came the harder challenge: developing a leadership programme that addressed the fundamental issue of ensuring participants gained confidence in their leadership capabilities, whilst creating a development environment where they felt respected, safe, valued and affirmed in their individual interests, talents, social identities, cultural values and backgrounds.

There are deep-rooted inequities in our education leadership systems. HDC needed to create a programme that embedded and promoted understanding, examined biases, built multicultural relationships and created inclusive communities — as well as meeting the programme's specification requirements.

§ 03 The approach

Coaching as the primary method.

Developing and delivering the programme involved close collaboration — with the programme consultant and the participants, with the client and college leadership, and with the accrediting body and subject-specialist associates.

Recognising that coaching is one of the best methods leaders can leverage to unleash their full potential, HDC adopted this approach as the key method for the support workshops. The work draws on the coaching expertise of Dr Helen Deane — together with her lived experience, and her established research interest in leadership and diversity.

HDC has developed a programme that supports participants to be in touch with the reality of the discriminatory experiences they have had and continue to have, to understand their strengths and weaknesses, and to develop a mindset and strategies to turn negative experiences and problems into opportunities.

Methods
  1. Programme co-developed with the college's external programme consultant
  2. Collaboration with college leadership, accrediting body and subject-specialist associates
  3. Coaching as the primary method for support workshops
  4. Use of Dr Helen Deane's coaching expertise, lived experience and leadership-diversity research
  5. Embedded examination of biases, multicultural relationships and inclusive community-building
  6. Clarity work on positive action vs. positive discrimination across leaders and participants
§ 04 Year one

Indications, not yet a full evaluation.

Being a relatively new programme, a full evaluation of the first year is not yet available. All indications, however, are that participants are progressing well and are motivated.

One candidate has achieved a promotion into a leadership position — which they have attributed to the support of HDC and the programme so far.

§ 05 Reflection

Positive action, named clearly, designed carefully.

The first work of any positive-action programme — before the modules, before the coaching, before the first cohort meeting — is to clear up what positive action is and is not, with everyone in the institution who will touch the work. Without that, the programme is fighting two battles at once: the substantive one, and the one with its own leadership's misunderstanding.

The second is to design a development environment in which participants can be honest about the discriminatory experiences they have had and continue to have — without making the work of describing those experiences become another tax on them. Coaching, used well, is one of the few methods that holds that tension at scale.
