§ 01 The brief

A consortium committed to growing its own leaders.

The college consortium is committed to developing its staff to become leaders. Building on more than fifteen years of success in leadership programmes for Future, Middle and Senior Managers, the client engaged HDC Training to develop and deliver the Future and Middle Managers Leadership and Management programme.

The Future and Middle Manager programmes have been running for twelve years. The colleges that invest in management training do so to make their leaders — and their organisations — more effective. That intent has stayed constant. The sector around it has not.

§ 02 The challenge

Continually developed to match the changing sixth-form sector.

The primary challenge is to continually develop the programme to address the leadership needs of the ever-changing sixth-form education sector — with the awareness that the colleges who have invested in the management training have done so to make their leaders and organisations more effective.

The constant updating and redevelopment of the content and delivery methods carries an additional requirement: that the content and participant support always address the ILM Level 5 certification specifications and accreditation requirements of the course.

Methods
  1. Programme development and delivery by HDC for the consortium
  2. Annual updating of content and delivery methods to match sector change
  3. Mapping to ILM Level 5 certification specifications throughout
  4. Collaboration with the client, college leadership and subject-specialist associates
  5. Collaboration with the accrediting body across cycles
  6. Use of Dr Helen Deane's leadership-development research interests
§ 03 The approach

Collaboration with client, accreditor and specialist associates.

Delivering these programmes involves collaboration with the client and the college leadership, as well as with the accrediting body and the subject-specialist associates HDC brings in for individual modules.

It also involves the expertise of Dr Helen Deane, whose research interests include — and continually return to — different ways of understanding and delivering leadership development programmes at all levels in the education sector.

Based on these elements, HDC continues to develop and deliver successful programmes with the client.

§ 04 What it has produced

A 100% pass rate, sustained.

HDC boasts a 100% pass rate for all participants who have completed the ILM accreditation through the programme — across twelve years of cohorts and continual content evolution.

The programme continues to run and continues to be refreshed. Each cycle is rebuilt to the sector that exists today, not the one that existed when the programme began.

§ 05 Reflection

Continual development is the discipline.

Long-running accredited programmes do not survive because the original design was right. They survive because the design is allowed to be wrong, in a slightly different way each year, and rebuilt against the sector that is actually in the room.

What this consortium has invested in — and what has paid back over twelve years of Future and Middle Manager cohorts — is the discipline of continual development against a moving accreditation standard, without ever letting the pass rate drift.
